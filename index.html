Frequently Asked Questions (FAQS)

A home inspection is carried out by our experienced and trusted inspectors, who carefully assess your property to identify any defects or signs of poor workmanship. This thorough evaluation covers the structural, mechanical, and electrical aspects of your home. Additionally, the inspection focuses on ensuring the property is safe and free from hazards. After the assessment, the inspector will provide the homeowner with a clear summary of any issues found, followed by a detailed report outlining the condition of the home

It’s highly recommended to inspect your house once you’ve collected the keys, activated utility services, and before starting any renovations. Without water and electricity, we won’t be able to properly test the mechanical systems or sanitary fittings. Conducting the inspection prior to renovation also helps prevent disputes with the developer over any claims related to defective work.

A home inspection is crucial, especially for new properties. Our skilled inspectors can identify poor workmanship and key defects, helping you address issues early and potentially lowering future repair costs.

Our base fee is a flat RM200, with no hidden charges after the service. The final cost may vary depending on factors such as property type, size, and location. Simply send us your request, and we'll provide a personalized quote.

Our inspection service adheres to the InterNACHI Standard of Practice and the Construction Industry Standard CIS 7:2014, a quality assessment system for building construction works established by the Construction Industry Development Board Malaysia (CIDB).

Our inspection service covers three key areas: architectural, mechanical, and electrical works. This includes a thorough evaluation of ceilings, internal and external wall surfaces, floors, doors, windows, staircases, fixtures, sanitary fittings, electrical systems, drainage, water leakage, and more.
